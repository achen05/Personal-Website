What are the ontological similarities between gender and electricity? The entire sculpture, excluding the custom-made frame, is a live, self-contained electrical circuit taking the form of a wardrobe. Each article of ‘clothing’ displayed is made out of metal wire which has been wrapped in aluminum foil then wrapped again with duct tape. They are deliberately mutated and bodiless, shifting in and out of definition depending on the viewer’s perspective and level of environmental light. There is a concealed power source that uses the wires inside the clothing to conduct electricity and power an LED light shaped like a candle; One of two charges is hidden with the power source while the other is running through the clothing, therefore making the clothing a necessary part of completing the electrical circuit. We cannot say that we see electrical currents, we can only see the things it powers; a light bulb in this case. In the same way, it’s not quite accurate to say that we can see gender in a physical, optical sense. Rather, we can only see the signifiers, performances, words, categories, etc. that gender materializes as. In the case of electricity and gender, both are defined, constrained, and perhaps even suffocated by the things they are forced to materialize as in order for us to use, communicate, and make sense of them.

 Using clay made from the fibers of newspapers as the primary material, Recompositions aims to explore the current state of information, data, media, and their respective institutions. It is a fallen structure created in response to a growing shift in the re-decentralization of the internet and the methods in which information and data are distributed and organized. It hopes to capture the poetry of the moments following the collapse of a monument, ready to be decomposed, recycled, and then reconstructed once more.

About

unedited :)
 Hi there! I'm Aldon and I'm comfortable with any pronouns. I was born in San Francisco and raised all around the Bay Area which unsurprisingly led to my ongoing fascination with all things tech. Combined with an early affinity for fine art and design, it seemed natural for me to explore the intersection between the three fields. I grew up jailbreaking and tweaking my iPhone, designing everything from bags to speculative technology to video games, and of course, drawing. Today, I'm particularly interested in designing and developing interfaces as they apply to digital, physical, and even abstract subjects. While I love design and aesthetic innovation, I'm currently focused primarily on the integrity of design, striving for ways to make interfaces honest, practical, and durable.
 I have special places in my heart for many things: low-tech, the Minecraft/BotW soundtrack, decentralized technology, and experimental electronic music among other things. When I'm not working on a project I'm most likely spending time with my family, thrift shopping with a friend, or reading (all that assigned critical theory, yall know what) at a nearby park.
