Container Theory
Every object is a container.
Objects are entities.
Entities can only be one of two types: tangible or intangible.
Objects are not tangible.
The only material aspects of an object are its parts.
Objects are more than the sum of its parts.
Objects necessarily consist of things: properties, actors, relations, etc.
The parts of an object, taken cumulatively, do not make an object but simply an array of parts.
Containers are intangible.
Tangible things are physical.
Containers are not physical.
Containers are schemas.
Schemas are a type of model.
Models are abstractions.
Abstractions are not physical.
Containers are a kind of technology.
Containers are schemas.
Schemas are a kind of technology.
Technology encompasses artificial meaans of interfacing with the world.
Schemas are a kind of model.
Models are abstract methods of organizing and understanding information about the world.
